About me

Email: ngernest@alumni.upenn.edu
GitHub: ngernest

Hi there! I'm a recent graduate of the University of Pennsylvania, where I obtained a Master's in Computer Science & a Bachelor's in Math & CS.

My academic interests include functional programming in OCaml & Haskell, proof assistants (eg. Coq), compilers and type systems. I also enjoy thinking about abstract algebra & graph theory.

At Penn, I was fortunate to be able to do programming languages research as part of PLClub:

  • I developed Mica, an automated tool for conducting property-based testing of OCaml modules, advised by Prof. Benjamin Pierce.
  • I also worked on a project mechanizing type soundness proofs for the Hindley-Milner type system in Coq, advised by Prof. Stephanie Weirich.

As an undergrad math major, I also conducted two independent studies where I learnt about:

  • Graphons & extremal graph theory (advised by Prof. Bhaswar Bhattacharya)
  • Applying electric network theory to random walks on graphs (via Penn’s Directed Reading Program)

I’ve also previously interned at Perpay & PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC), where I worked on data engineering
& web security projects respectively.
